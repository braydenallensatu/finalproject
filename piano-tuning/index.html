Education

Why Does Tuning Matter?

A piano out of tune is more than just an annoyance — it quietly chips away at tone quality, pitch stability, and the playing experience for everyone who sits at the bench. Whether you're a serious student or a family with a piano in the living room, regular tuning keeps your instrument sounding and feeling the way it was designed to.

  • Richer, clearer sound with natural resonance across every note
  • Stable pitch so chords ring true and melodies stay clean
  • More responsive, consistent feel under the fingers
  • Reduced long-term stress on strings and the soundboard
  • A better learning environment for students developing their ear

What happens without regular tuning?
Over time, string tension drifts and the piano settles below concert pitch. The longer it goes without tuning, the harder it becomes to bring back — sometimes requiring a pitch raise or multiple sessions to restore it properly.

Guidelines

When Should You Tune Your Piano?

There's no single rule that fits every piano, but here's what experience has taught me about staying ahead of it.

  • 1.
    At least once a year
    Even a piano that isn't played often will drift in pitch due to temperature and humidity changes. An annual tuning keeps it stable and ready.
  • 2.
    Twice a year for active players
    Teachers, frequent players, and families with children taking lessons will notice the difference with fall and spring tunings.
  • 3.
    After any move
    Moving stresses the instrument and changes its environment. Allow 2–4 weeks to acclimate, then schedule a tuning.
  • 4.
    After significant climate shifts
    Wide swings in humidity or temperature — especially near vents or exterior walls — can call for more frequent tuning.
